  • 11. Gambyong Dance Gambyong dance is a dance to welcome guests or start a wedding reception. This dance is named after the dancer named Gambyong. This dancer lived during the time of Sunan Paku Buwana IV in Surakarta. He was proficient in dancing and had a melodious voice so that he became the idol of the youth at that time. Gambyong dance choreography is mostly centered on the use of footwork, body, arms, and head. The dancer not only flexes his body, but also has to be skilled. There are various movements in the Gambyong dance. The srisig movement is a movement with a standing position on tiptoe followed by small steps. Nacah oblique, namely the left foot moves to the side, alternately followed by the right foot placed in front of the left foot. Kengser is the movement of the foot sideways by shifting with the foot position still close to the floor. Embat or entrag motion is a movement with the knee open because it moves downwards and upwards. In addition, there is also a walking movement (now mlaku), and motion in place (now mandheg). Subject : English

Category : Tenses (Past Simple and Past Continuous)

Level : JHS VIII

Keywords : when, while

Code : 5

Past Simple Tense is used to describe an event that happened and completed in the past.

Pattern : S + Preterite (v₂)

The past continuous tense is used to describe an event that was happening round a specific time in the past.

Pattern : S + was/were + present participle

Past continuous and past simple are often used together. The past continuous describes the longer or background action while the past simple describes the action that interrupts the background action.

ANSWERS :

While Mrs Rina WAS READING her little boy a strory, he FELL asleep so she CLOSED the book and quietly TIPTOED out of the room.

11. Gambyong Dance Gambyong dance is a dance to welcome guests or start a wedding reception. This dance is named after the dancer named Gambyong. This dancer lived during the time of Sunan Paku Buwana IV in Surakarta. He was proficient in dancing and had a melodious voice so that he became the idol of the youth at that time. Gambyong dance choreography is mostly centered on the use of footwork, body, arms, and head. The dancer not only flexes his body, but also has to be skilled. There are various movements in the Gambyong dance. The srisig movement is a movement with a standing position on tiptoe followed by small steps. Nacah oblique, namely the left foot moves to the side, alternately followed by the right foot placed in front of the left foot. Kengser is the movement of the foot sideways by shifting with the foot position still close to the floor. Embat or entrag motion is a movement with the knee open because it moves downwards and upwards. In addition, there is also a walking movement (now mlaku), and motion in place (now mandheg).
